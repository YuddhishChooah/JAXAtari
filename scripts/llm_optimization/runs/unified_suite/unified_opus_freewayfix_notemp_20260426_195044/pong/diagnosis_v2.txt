## 1. Root cause
The best `predict_gain` was learned to be **negative (-1.36)**, which inverts the vertical prediction direction. Combined with a small **negative aim_offset (-0.39)**, the policy is not actually intercepting via prediction — it is loosely tracking the ball's current Y with reversed dynamics. The policy still wins because the enemy AI is weak, but it is leaving most of its potential performance on the table.

## 2. Evidence from metrics
- `interception_rate = 0.0`: the paddle is essentially never in the predicted intercept window.
- `avg_ball_tracking_error ≈ 24.7`, `max ≈ 162`: tracking is loose and occasionally totally lost.
- `paddle_jitter_count ≈ 112` per episode despite a generous `dead_zone≈2.76`: indicates the *target itself* oscillates frame-to-frame (consistent with using raw `dy * t * gain` with noisy single-frame `dy`).
- `points_lost = 0` but `points_won ≈ 12.7` over 4000 frames → very long rallies (`avg_rally_length ≈ 307`); the agent stalls instead of finishing points.
- Negative learned `predict_gain` is a strong signal that the prediction term is being used as a *contrarian* signal, not a real intercept — the optimizer found a degenerate local optimum.

## 3. Policy logic causing it
- `_predict_intercept` uses single-frame `dy` and a learnable `gain`. With noisy `dy` and the wall-reflection folding, prediction is unstable; the optimizer compensated by flipping `gain` sign.
- `target_when_away = by` (raw ball Y) when `dx <= 0`: during the long return phase, the paddle chases the ball backward across the screen instead of returning to a strategic ready position. This inflates rally length and tracking error.
- `aim_offset` is applied uniformly; there is no mechanism to deliberately hit off-center to end rallies (insight #5 unused).
- `fire_bias` is a single scalar gating FIRE globally — it does not modulate FIRE near contact, so it adds no tactical value.
- Dead zone is symmetric and constant; no hysteresis, so jitter persists when target wobbles.
- Prediction uses `bx` (current) and `dx` from one frame; the division `dist/dx` blows up for small `dx` and the `1e-3` clamp produces huge `t`, then folded — this is what made negative gain attractive.

## 4. What to preserve
- The 52-feature parsing and use of `prev`/`curr` to derive `dx, dy`.
- The compact parameter set (dead_zone, aim_offset, fallback_y, predict_gain, fire_bias) and overall structure.
- Wall-reflection folding for the predicted intercept.
- Action mapping (2/3 and 4/5) and the `use_fire` selector pattern.
- Returning to a fallback Y when the ball is inactive.

## 5. What to change next
- **Gate prediction on `dx > 0` only**, and when `dx <= 0` set target to a *ready position* (e.g., `fallback_y` or a learnable `idle_y`), not the ball's current Y. This will cut jitter and rally length.
- **Constrain `predict_gain` to be positive** (e.g., `softplus` or fix to 1.0 and instead learn a small `dy_scale`) so the optimizer cannot settle on the inverted regime.
- **Smooth `dy`/`dx`**: average over the two frames you have, or clamp `t` to a maximum horizon so a near-zero `dx` doesn't explode predictions.
- **Add hysteresis**: separate `dead_zone_enter` and `dead_zone_exit`, or freeze action when `|error|` is within dead zone *and* previous action was idle. Reduces the 112 reversals/episode.
- **Strategic aim**: make `aim_offset` depend on sign of `dy` (or on enemy Y) so returns are deliberately steep — this targets insight #5 and should shorten rallies dramatically.
- **Conditional FIRE**: only emit FIRE-combo when the ball is close to the player (e.g., `PLAYER_X - bx < threshold`) instead of globally; replace `fire_bias` scalar with a learnable distance threshold.
- **Learnable `idle_y`** distinct from `fallback_y` (ball inactive vs. ball receding) — current single fallback conflates two regimes.
- Consider clamping the predicted target to `[PLAYFIELD_TOP, PLAYFIELD_BOTTOM - PADDLE_HEIGHT]` *after* aim_offset, so off-center aiming never drives the paddle into a wall and stalls.
